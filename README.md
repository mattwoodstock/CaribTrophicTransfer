# Trophic Transfer model for island-specific ecosystem-based assessments in the U.S. Caribbean

Here are the main analytical panels and visualizations for the proposed portfolio-type approach to managing U.S. Caribbean fisheries.

## 1. Assessment Phase
![Figure 1. Example analytical outputs from the Assessment Phase. Panels illustrate ecosystem Maximum Sustainable Yield (MSY) estimates derived from trophic transfer models (Top Left), energy utilization ratio indicators evaluating ecosystem overfishing limits (Bottom Left), historical gear composition (Top Right), and the resulting minimum and maximum biologically and economically sustainable effort thresholds by gear and island platform (Bottom Right).
](./R/Plots/SEDAR103_Assessment_Phase_Panel.png)

## 2. Implementation & Tracking Phase
![Figure 2. Example monitoring dashboards for the Implementation and Tracking Phase. These panels demonstrate the annual monitoring of fishing effort against predefined sustainable limits (Top Left), tracking of holistic ecosystem and socioeconomic indicators (Bottom Left), and species abundance trajectories relative to action triggers (Top Right). The management scorecard (Bottom Right) summarizes the probability of meeting predefined performance goals to guide timely adaptive management interventions.](./R/Plots/SEDAR103_Implementation_Phase_Panel.png)

## 3. Scenario Forecasting & Management Strategy Evaluation (MSE)
![Figure 3. Forecasting and Management Strategy Evaluation (MSE) summaries. Simulating fleet and ecosystem dynamics over a 10-year projection allows managers to evaluate end-state performance trade-offs across biological and socioeconomic goals (Top), anticipate the frequency of specific trigger breaches under varying environmental and economic pressures (Bottom Left), and visualize the timeline of required management interventions (Bottom Right).](./R/Plots/SEDAR103_Scenario_Summary_Panel.png)
